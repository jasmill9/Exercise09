# EX09 Data Analysis - Continuous Improvement

## Analysis Summary

For this project, I analyzed whether COMP110 should slow down its pace 
for students with no prior coding experience. I hypothesized that students 
with less experience would report lower understanding ratings and rate the 
course pace as too fast compared to students with more experience.

To explore this, I loaded survey data from COMP110 students and grouped 
them into three categories based on prior coding experience: No Experience, 
Some Experience (2-12 months), and Advanced (1+ years). I then compared 
their self-reported understanding ratings across these groups.

## Conclusion

My analysis was inconclusive in supporting the idea that the course should 
slow down its pace for students with no prior coding experience. Students 
with no prior experience did not vary significantly from those with some 
experience in their understanding ratings. Both groups rated their 
understanding around 4-5 on average. However, advanced students were 
proportionally more likely to rate their understanding above 5, suggesting 
some relationship between prior experience and understanding, though not 
strong enough to draw firm conclusions.

To build more confidence, future surveys could ask students directly whether 
the pace felt appropriate for their experience level. If findings were more 
conclusive, slowing the pace for beginners could negatively impact more 
experienced students who may find the course too slow, and restructuring 
course pacing would require significant effort from instructors and staff.
